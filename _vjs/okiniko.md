**Okiniko** is the artist the mapping community calls when the space itself is the problem — and the solution. Where most projection artists require a flat white wall and a clean sightline, Okiniko specialises in the opposite: complex architectural interiors, unusual geometries, historic venues with pillars and vaulted ceilings, club spaces that were never designed with projection in mind.

Working primarily in **MadMapper** and **VDMX**, with a hardware stack anchored by the **Matrox FX4** and **Kinect V2** for depth sensing, Okiniko constructs 360-degree projection environments that conform precisely to the surfaces they inhabit — turning cathedrals into abstract digital landscapes, club ceilings into living skies, pillars into reactive light instruments.

This is technically demanding work. A 360-degree mapping in a complex interior requires solving problems that have no precedent — every new venue is a new puzzle. Okiniko's decade of experience across European mapping festivals has produced an encyclopedic knowledge of how light, projection angle, and architectural surface interact.

In 2026, Okiniko's reputation as the definitive specialist for spatially complex immersive setups remains unchallenged.
