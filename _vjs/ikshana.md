**Ikshana** is an artist whose work occupies a singular space in live visual performance — where ancient visual traditions of mandala and sacred geometry meet the raw generativity of live feedback systems. Her performances feel less like a VJ set and more like a ritual: something being summoned rather than played.

The technical foundation is the **camera feedback loop** — a system where a camera points at a screen displaying the camera's own output, creating recursive, self-generating imagery that evolves continuously and unpredictably. In the hands of most practitioners this produces noise. In Ikshana's hands, it produces psychedelic, mandala-like organic forms that feel genuinely alive.

This is not a comfortable or predictable process. Feedback systems are inherently unstable — they can collapse into chaos or crystallise into unexpected orders at any moment. Ikshana performs within this instability, guiding rather than controlling the visual output, creating performances that are genuinely unrepeatable.

Recognised in the **VJ Academy** community as a leader in her approach, Ikshana brings a philosophical depth to live visual performance that is rare in any context.
