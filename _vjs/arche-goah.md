**Arche Goah** has built a reputation as one of the most distinctive visual artists working in generative performance today. Their practice is defined by a rejection of the angular and the static — instead, Arche Goah pursues forms that feel biological, that shift and breathe and pulse as if generated by organic rather than computational processes.

Their concept of "visual morphologies" describes this approach precisely: the performance is not the playing of loops or the triggering of effects, but the live evolution of generative organisms that respond to music, environment, and the energy of the room. The shapes that emerge feel less designed than *grown*.

AI systems are central to this practice — not as a novelty but as a genuine creative partner. Machine learning models trained on biological forms, growth patterns, and fluid dynamics produce the raw material that Arche Goah shapes and performs in real time.

They are frequently booked for live cinema performances and immersive club nights where the visual material is expected to evolve continuously across hours of performance.
