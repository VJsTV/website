**Yannick Jacquet** is a Belgian visual artist and one of the most important figures in the history of projection art. As a founding member of **AntiVJ**, he helped define an aesthetic approach to projection mapping that prioritised geometry, material truth, and architectural logic over spectacle — an approach that has been enormously influential on practitioners worldwide.

Jacquet's work uses light as a sculptural material. Rather than projecting images *onto* surfaces, he projects light *with* surfaces — responding to the specific geometry, material, and spatial logic of each environment he works with. The result is a practice that feels simultaneously very simple (a projection, some light) and profoundly sophisticated in its formal intelligence.

His installations have been presented at **Ars Electronica**, **Nuit Blanche**, and major venues across Europe, Asia, and North America. His work with AntiVJ has been credited with fundamentally changing the visual language of projection art internationally.
