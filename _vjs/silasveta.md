**SilaSveta** (Сила Света, meaning "Power of Light" in Russian) is one of Moscow's most celebrated creative studios working with projection mapping and immersive light art. Since their founding, they have produced major works for festivals, brands, and cultural institutions across Russia and internationally.

Their practice is defined by a commitment to narrative and participation: rather than simply spectacular visual effects, SilaSveta create works where audiences become part of the story. *KOLOBOK* — their interactive retelling of a classic Slavic folk tale — exemplifies this approach, using gesture-recognition technology to invite festival visitors to shape the projection's narrative in real time.

SilaSveta have produced work for the Moscow International Festival of Light (Circle of Light), major Russian and European brands, and cultural institutions, cementing their reputation as one of Russia's most technically accomplished and conceptually ambitious studios.
