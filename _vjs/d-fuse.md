**D-Fuse** is a London-based audiovisual collective founded by **Mike Faulkner** in the mid-1990s. Over nearly three decades, D-Fuse has been among the most consistently influential forces in VJ culture and live visual arts — pioneering live cinema as a performance discipline long before the term existed.

Their work spans music videos, installation, live performance, and public art, always driven by the same core impulse: the moving image as a plastic, live material to be performed rather than merely played back. Works like *Particle* — premiered at the On_Off Festival in São Paulo — exemplify their signature approach: rigorous in concept, visceral in experience.

D-Fuse have collaborated with musicians, choreographers, and technologists across the world, and have been commissioned by major institutions and festivals including the Barbican, Sonar, and Ars Electronica. Their publication *VJ: Audio-Visual Art and VJ Culture* remains a defining reference work for the field.
