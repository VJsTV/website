**Néstor Lizalde** is a Spanish-born artist and researcher based in London whose practice investigates what computational systems can see, and what they cannot. Working with machine learning, computer vision, and neural networks, Lizalde creates artworks that expose the specific logic of computational vision — revealing the assumptions, biases, and genuine strangeness embedded in how machines represent the visual world.

His practice combines technical research rigour with a strong visual intelligence — producing work that is simultaneously a contribution to the discourse around AI and visual culture and a genuinely compelling aesthetic proposition.
