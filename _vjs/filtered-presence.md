**Filtered Presence** — the duo of **Atoraye** and **p0x0** — make visual performance work in which the audience is the content. Using thermal cameras and depth sensors positioned throughout the performance space, they capture the heat signatures and silhouettes of every person in the room and integrate that data in real-time into the visual layer of their performance.

The effect is unsettling and beautiful simultaneously: the crowd becomes a ghostly digital presence on the screen, their thermal traces smeared, ghosted, and transformed by processing into something that looks like echoes of bodies rather than bodies themselves. The audience watches itself be rendered, watching its own presence filtered through a technological lens.

This is "Digital Ghost" aesthetics — the visual language of presence-as-trace, of bodies captured not by conventional cameras but by instruments that see heat, depth, and mass rather than colour and light.

Atoraye and p0x0 were selected for the prestigious **25AV Residency Programme** (Athens, Brussels, Milan, Munich) for 2026–2027, recognising the originality and rigour of their practice.
