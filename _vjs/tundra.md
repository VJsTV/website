**Tundra** is an international collective that consistently makes work that redefines what a "screen" can be. Where most installation artists work with LED panels, projection, or conventional display technology, Tundra starts with a fundamentally different question: what if the display is made of grass? Of water? Of fog? Of thousands of suspended points of light?

Their touring work *The Day We Left Field* is the definitive statement of this approach in 2026: an inverted meadow of thousands of blades of artificial grass, each acting as a physical pixel in a massive, glowing, ever-changing image. The piece exists at the intersection of natural and artificial, creating an environment that feels simultaneously organic and deeply technological.

Tundra are the collective that festivals call when they want a "world first" — a visual experience that cannot be categorised or compared to what has existed before. Their technical inventiveness and conceptual ambition operate at the same level, producing work that is as intellectually rigorous as it is visually extraordinary.
