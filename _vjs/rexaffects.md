**RexAffects** is the leading figure in what the VJ community has come to call "Gamified VJing" — an approach to live visual performance that borrows its control paradigm from first-person video games, treating the VJ set as a live 3D environment to be navigated in real time.

Using **Notch** for real-time 3D rendering and an **Xbox controller** as the primary instrument, RexAffects moves through fully volumetric 3D worlds in sync with the music — the camera angles, depth of field, and environmental lighting all controlled live, with the same reflexive speed and physical precision as competitive gaming.

The result is visual performance that genuinely cannot be pre-programmed or replicated: each set is a live improvisation through a three-dimensional space, shaped by the music and the energy of the moment. When the track drops, the camera moves. When the breakdown arrives, the world slows.

RexAffects has demonstrated that the tools and instincts of gaming and the art of VJing are more closely related than anyone previously articulated — and in doing so, has opened up a new direction for the entire field.
