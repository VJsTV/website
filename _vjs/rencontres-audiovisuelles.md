**Rencontres Audiovisuelles** is a Lille-based organisation whose influence on global architectural projection mapping extends far beyond the productions they create. As the organisers of the **Video Mapping Festival** — one of the world's leading events dedicated to architectural projection — they are simultaneously practitioners, curators, educators, and gatekeepers of an art form.

The Video Mapping Festival takes place annually in Lille and has grown into an international event that attracts leading mapping studios and artists from across Europe, the Americas, and Asia. Rencontres Audiovisuelles curate the festival's programme with a philosophy that balances spectacular public spectacle with support for experimental and emerging practice.

Beyond the festival, the organisation runs residency and talent development programmes, workshop series, and international cultural exchanges — making them a genuine infrastructure for the development of the art form globally, not merely a promoter or production company. Their commitment to supporting new artists has made them responsible for the careers of dozens of now-prominent mapping practitioners.
