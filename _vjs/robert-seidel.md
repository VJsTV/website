**Robert Seidel** is a Berlin-based artist whose work occupies a singular position in contemporary visual art: the space between generative film, projection sculpture, and expanded cinema. His practice dissolves conventional categories — his pieces are neither quite paintings, nor films, nor sculptures, but something that contains aspects of all three.

In works like *Tearing Shadows* (2013), Seidel positions projectors and physical sculptures in precise spatial relationships, so that light and shadow become equally expressive materials. Viewers move through environments where the image is not confined to a screen but distributed through space — across walls, floors, and sculptural surfaces simultaneously.

His generative imagery — produced with custom software that Seidel develops and refines over long periods — has a distinctive quality: organic and precise, mathematical and painterly. Each frame is unique, yet the work has a strong visual identity that is immediately recognisable.

Seidel has exhibited at major institutions and festivals internationally, including Ars Electronica, Transmediale, and numerous gallery contexts in Europe and North America.
