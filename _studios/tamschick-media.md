**Tamschick Media+Space** are a Berlin studio whose work addresses the deepest integration possible between architecture and media — permanent installations in which digital imagery, sound, and physical space become genuinely inseparable.

Where many studios apply media to architecture as a surface treatment, Tamschick Media+Space design spaces in which the media and the architecture emerge together. Exhibition halls, museum galleries, visitor centres — these spaces are conceived from the outset as environments where screens, projections, and physical materials work as a unified system.

Their approach is called "scenographic media design" — a term that precisely captures their position between set design, exhibition design, and media art. The result is permanent installations that serve institutional purposes (visitor engagement, educational content, historical narration) while achieving the spatial qualities of art.

Major clients include cultural institutions, heritage sites, and science museums across Europe. Their work is experienced by millions of visitors annually in permanent installations that are designed to function, evolve, and remain compelling over decades rather than days.
