**Studio DRIFT** — founded by **Lonneke Gordijn** and **Ralph Nauta** in Amsterdam — creates work at the intersection of biological intelligence and industrial engineering. Their practice is defined by a central question: what happens when natural systems — the way a flock of birds moves, the way a flower opens in light — are translated into engineered objects and systems?

Their answer has produced some of the most extraordinary kinetic works of the last decade. **Shylight** — a series of luminous sculptures that open and close via industrial winch systems, exactly mimicking the light-responsive behaviour of certain flowers — is both scientifically accurate and profoundly moving. **Franchise Freedom** uses hundreds of synchronised drones to recreate the emergent behaviour of a starling murmuration in the night sky.

Their 2026 work *Wind of Change* continues this trajectory: a massive outdoor drone performance in which light-emitting drones form three-dimensional sculptures in the night sky, their formations driven by algorithms modelled on natural swarm behaviour.

Studio DRIFT have exhibited at the **Stedelijk Museum** Amsterdam, **Saatchi Gallery** London, **Art Basel** Miami, and have been commissioned by major cultural institutions and public art programmes worldwide.
