**WHITEvoid** — the studio of **Christopher Bauder** — operates at the absolute frontier of kinetic light art, producing installations in which hundreds of winch-powered mirrors, LED nodes, and laser systems move in precise choreography with music and visual computation.

Their Berlin studio space, **DARK MATTER**, occupies a 1,000m² former industrial factory in East Berlin — one of the most significant physical assets of any studio in the field. The space houses **TRANSFORMATOR**: a permanent installation of hundreds of motorised elements that serves simultaneously as a completed artwork and as a testing environment for new systems.

*SKALAR* (2018, Kraftwerk Berlin), created with Robert Henke, used 32 laser projectors and 64 robotic mirror units to create a physical experience of light that audiences described as overwhelming in the most productive sense. *VEKTOR* extended this approach into new territory.

In 2026, WHITEvoid / DARK MATTER remains the global reference point for the integration of physical kinetic systems with digital visual and sonic computation — work that sits precisely at the boundary between sculpture, architecture, and live performance.
