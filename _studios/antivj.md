**AntiVJ** is less a studio than a philosophy — a European visual label founded by artists including **Joanie Lemercier**, **Yannick Jacquet** (Jake), **Romain Tardy**, and **Nonotak** that became the defining influence on a generation of projection mapping artists worldwide.

Their aesthetic is immediately recognisable: geometric precision, mathematical elegance, radical restraint. Where commercial studios reach for spectacle, AntiVJ reaches for clarity — works in which a single precisely-placed line of light on an architectural surface can be more powerful than a thousand simultaneous effects.

The label operates as a collective of independent practitioners rather than a conventional studio — each artist maintains their own practice while remaining part of the AntiVJ constellation. This model has influenced how the broader art world thinks about collectives and authorship in digital media art.

AntiVJ projects have been presented at major festivals and institutions worldwide, from **Mutek** to the **Centre Pompidou**. Their influence on the vocabulary of projection mapping and generative visual art cannot be overstated.
