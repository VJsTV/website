**59 Productions** are the definitive studio at the intersection of theatre and projection design — a London and New York-based practice that has produced some of the most celebrated large-scale visual events in the world over the past two decades.

Where many studios approach projection as a primarily technological challenge, 59 Productions approach it as a narrative one. Their roots in theatrical design mean that every projection system they build serves a dramatic or storytelling purpose — light and image in service of meaning, not spectacle for its own sake.

Their landmark commissions include projections on the **Sydney Opera House** sails, the **Tower of London** moat (for the *Blood Swept Lands and Seas of Red* poppy installation), and major productions at the **Metropolitan Opera** in New York. They also produced the opening ceremony sequences for the **2012 London Olympics**.

In the theatre world, they have designed projection and visual environments for productions at the **National Theatre**, **RSC**, and Broadway — making them the rare studio equally at home on a festival mainstage and in a West End house.
