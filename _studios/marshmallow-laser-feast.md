**Marshmallow Laser Feast** (MLF) are the UK's most celebrated immersive experience studio — a practice whose work consistently transcends conventional categories to create encounters that feel less like "art exhibitions" and more like alterations of perception.

Their defining methodology is the making-visible of the invisible: using LiDAR scanners, biological sensors, breath-capture systems, and spatial audio to reveal the hidden dimensions of the natural and scientific world. In *We Live in an Ocean of Air* (2019), audiences could literally see the oxygen generated by a tree as a glowing cloud of molecules, tracked by sensors measuring their own breath. The experience was simultaneously scientifically accurate and profoundly moving.

Their approach to technology is integrative rather than additive — they use LiDAR not as a gimmick but because it captures the world in a way that conventional cameras cannot, revealing depth, mass, and spatial relationships that are otherwise invisible.

MLF have exhibited at the **Saatchi Gallery**, **Eden Project**, **Barbican**, **SXSW**, and venues worldwide. In 2026, they continue to develop work at the frontier of environmental immersion and multi-sensory experience design.
