In 2026, the landscape of **Studios** has moved beyond traditional production offices. These spaces are now "Phygital Labs"—physical environments designed to stress-test real-time code, massive projection rays, and human-machine interaction.

To build out the **Studios** section for **VJs TV**, here are the most important hubs where the world's visuals are being "born" right now.

---

### 1. The European Powerhouses (Modular & Industrial)

* **WHITEvoid / DARK MATTER (Berlin, Germany):**
* *The Vibe:* A massive former factory in East Berlin turned into a permanent playground for light.
* *The Space:* Founded by **Christopher Bauder**, this studio features the **TRANSFORMATOR**, a 1,000 $m^2$ hall used to prototype shows like *SKALAR* and *VEKTOR*.
* *Why it matters:* This is the gold standard for **Kinetic Light Art**—where hundreds of winch-powered mirrors move in sync with visuals and sound.


* **Rencontres Audiovisuelles / Video Mapping Festival Lab (Lille, France):**
* *The Vibe:* The "Oxford" of VJing.
* *The Space:* A dedicated lab supported by the French government that provides high-end projectors and mapping software to independent artists.
* *Why it matters:* It’s the primary training ground for architectural mapping in Europe, keeping the "VJ" craft alive through constant workshops and a massive annual festival.



### 2. The Artistic Research Hubs (Data & AI)

* **Ouchhh Studio (Istanbul / Global):**
* *The Vibe:* A "Machine Intelligence" playground.
* *The Space:* Based in Istanbul with branches in Berlin and LA, their studio focuses on **Neuro-Design**. They literally plug musicians into EEG headsets to drive the studio's generative render farms.
* *Why it matters:* They are pioneers of "Data Sculptures"—turning things like Istanbul's wind patterns or NASA's space data into massive public installations.


* **404.zero (Nomadic / St. Petersburg / LA):**
* *The Vibe:* Dark, mathematical, and modular.
* *The Space:* Known for their "No-Loop" philosophy. Their "studio" is often a dark room filled with modular synthesizers and custom-coded **TouchDesigner** patches.
* *Why it matters:* They are the masters of "Arrival"—massive, strobe-heavy installations that use math to create visuals that feel like they are tearing through the fabric of the room.



### 3. The New Immersion Centers (Domes & XR)

* **Wisdome / Tekniska Museet (Stockholm, Sweden):**
* *The Vibe:* The ultimate "Full-Dome" laboratory.
* *The Space:* A world-leading dome facility that hosts the **Immersive Dome Lab**. It’s a research space for VJs and creative technologists to test 360-degree spatial visuals and 3D sound design (using **Ableton** and **TouchDesigner**).
* *Why it matters:* It has moved dome VJing from "planetarium shows" into "high-art live performances."


* **IVSL (Immersive Visualisation and Simulation Lab - Norwich, UK):**
* *The Vibe:* A hardcore tech-spec lab.
* *The Space:* Features a **4D Holosys Volumetric Capture System** and a 10m x 4m 360-degree LED volume.
* *Why it matters:* This is where "Virtual VJing" is born. Artists use this lab to capture human movement in 3D and project it into "Metaverse" or live concert environments.



### 4. High-End Collective Studios

* **Tundra (International):**
* *The Vibe:* Synesthetic experimentation.
* *The Space:* Their 2026 solo exhibitions (like those in Spain) showcase the culmination of their "Multimedia Synesthesia" lab work. They focus on how light, sound, and *haptics* (vibration) can trigger altered states of perception.


* **Moment Factory (Montreal / Tokyo):**
* *The Vibe:* The "Hollywood" of Visuals.
* *The Space:* Massive "Black Box" studios where they can set up 1:1 scale replicas of stadium stages to test visuals for artists like **Madonna** or the **Las Vegas Sphere**.
